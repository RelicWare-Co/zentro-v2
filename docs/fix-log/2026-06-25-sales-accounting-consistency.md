# Sales Accounting Consistency

## Symptom

Customers reported accounting values that did not reconcile across sales, credit,
shift closeout, inventory, and dashboard views. The highest-impact mismatches
were cash overpayments being shown as sale paid revenue, account-level credit
payments reducing customer balance without settling the related sales, and sale
cancellations depending on mutable product inventory settings.

## Root Cause

- Cash payments stored the tendered amount, but sale projections and dashboard
  payment mix treated that tendered amount as money applied to the sale.
- Credit payments without `saleId` were allowed for legacy balances, but the same
  path was available for accounts created by credit sales. It reduced
  `creditAccount.balance` while leaving sale payment rows/statuses unchanged.
- Sale cancellation restored stock from current sale items and current
  `product.trackInventory` instead of reversing the sale inventory movements that
  actually changed stock at sale time.
- Cancellation changed `sale.status` after side effects, leaving concurrent
  cancellation calls able to perform duplicate stock/credit reversals.
- Credit sale cancellation reduced account balance but left no offsetting ledger
  transaction.
- Paid-sale cancellation implicitly removed collected payments from closeout and
  dashboard accounting without a persisted refund/reversal record.
- Credit payments calculated per-sale balances before writing account/payment
  changes, leaving a concurrency window for two payments to over-allocate the
  same sale when the customer had multiple outstanding debts.
- The restaurant table checkout receipt rebuilt `paidAmount` from tendered cash
  instead of the amount applied to the sale, so a cash-change receipt could show
  more paid than the sale total.

## Solution

- Cap sale `paidAmount` at `sale.totalAmount` for sale creation responses,
  sales list rows, and sale detail rows. Raw payment rows still preserve tendered
  cash for drawer/change accounting.
- Recompute dashboard payment mix from payment rows with the same cash-change
  logic used by shift closeout, so returned change is not counted as collected
  cash.
- Allocate account-level credit payments to outstanding credit-sale balances
  before falling back to a legacy unassigned payment row.
- Make cancellation claim the sale with a conditional `status != cancelled`
  update before side effects.
- Restore stock from the original `inventory_movement` rows generated by the
  sale.
- Insert a `reversal` credit transaction when cancelling a credit sale.
- Reject tracked-product sales that would push stock below zero.
- Reject cancellation of any sale that already has payment rows. Until the app
  has an explicit refund entity/workflow, paid sales must not disappear from
  accounting by status change alone.
- Lock the targeted sale row before recalculating its outstanding balance, and
  update the credit account before automatic payment allocation so concurrent
  credit payments serialize against current balances.
- Share the applied-paid calculation with POS table receipts so displayed
  `paidAmount` is capped at `sale.totalAmount` while raw payment rows still
  preserve tendered cash.
